Ladakh — Melting Glaciers

Parkachik glacier and cryosphere impacts
0 m/yr
Recent field-observed retreat

Key points

  • Parkachik retreat increased from ~2 m/yr (1971–1999) to ~12 m/yr (1999–2015) with spikes ~20 m/yr recently. (Annals of Glaciology; Wadia Institute)
  • Black carbon & dust lower albedo, increasing melt. (field & remote-sensing studies)
  • Proglacial lakes and moraine instability create GLOF risk. (govt & institute assessments)
  • Immediate: flood events during melts. Long-term: reduced baseflow for late-season water.
Short actions
  • Support lake monitoring & mapping.
  • Promote cleaner fuels and reduce biomass burning.

Local impacts

Villages and irrigation rely on glacier runoff; storage & early warning reduce risks.

Western Ghats — River Pollution

Nethravathi, Sharavathi and connected systems
Biodiversity hotspot

Quick findings

  • Microplastics (fibers & fragments) detected in sediments & waters. (peer-reviewed)
  • Nutrient runoff (N & P) drives algal blooms & oxygen dips. (monitoring)
  • Drivers: sewage, tourism waste, agri runoff, mining. (KSPCB)
  • Bioaccumulation raises contaminants in fish — public health risk.

Pressure points

Sewage, pilgrimage tourism, fertilizer runoff, and mining reduce water quality and habitat.

Microplastics — field notes

Fibers primarily originate from synthetic textiles (polyester, nylon). Fragments mainly derive from plastic packaging degradation. Microplastics act as vectors for hydrophobic contaminants (PCBs, PAHs) and can transfer through trophic levels, with concentrations up to 5 particles/L in water and 100 particles/kg in sediment.

Suggested interventions

Implement tertiary sewage treatment with microfiltration (≤10μm). Establish riparian buffer zones (30-50m width) with native vegetation. Develop community-based water quality monitoring using low-cost sensors. Pilot constructed wetlands for agricultural runoff treatment.

Solution Implementation Timeline

Phased approach to crisis mitigation
Short-term (1-2 years)

Immediate Actions

  • Ladakh: Install GLOF early-warning systems, promote clean cooking stoves
  • Western Ghats: Enforce existing pollution regulations, establish waste collection at tourist sites
  • Cost: ~₹50 crore | Responsible: State disaster management, pollution control boards
Medium-term (3-5 years)

Infrastructure Development

  • Ladakh: Construct ice stupas and reservoirs, expand glacier monitoring network
  • Western Ghats: Upgrade sewage treatment plants, restore riparian buffers
  • Cost: ~₹200 crore | Responsible: Central water ministry, state governments
Long-term (5+ years)

Systemic Transformation

  • Ladakh: Transition to renewable energy, implement regional climate adaptation plan
  • Western Ghats: Develop circular economy for waste, establish ecological monitoring network
  • Cost: ~₹500 crore | Responsible: National climate mission, environment ministry

Key Findings

  • Without intervention, Parkachik Glacier could lose 60% of its current mass by 2050
  • With aggressive mitigation, glacier loss could be limited to 30% by 2050
  • Western Ghats rivers could achieve "good" water quality status by 2035 with full implementation of proposed solutions
  • Biodiversity recovery in Western Ghats may take 10-15 years after pollution control measures are fully implemented

Glossary

Key terms explained

Albedo

The measure of how much solar radiation is reflected by a surface. Ice and snow have high albedo, while water and rock have low albedo.

BOD (Biochemical Oxygen Demand)

The amount of dissolved oxygen needed by aerobic biological organisms to break down organic material in water. Higher BOD indicates more pollution.

GLOF (Glacial Lake Outburst Flood)

A sudden release of water from a glacial lake, often caused by dam failure or avalanche. Can cause catastrophic flooding downstream.

Microplastics

Plastic particles less than 5mm in size, originating from degradation of larger plastic items or manufactured as microbeads.

Riparian Buffer

Vegetated areas adjacent to rivers and streams that help filter pollutants, stabilize banks, and provide habitat.

Trophic Levels

The positions organisms occupy in a food chain. Microplastics can transfer between trophic levels through consumption.

Black Carbon

Particulate matter produced by incomplete combustion of fossil fuels and biomass. Deposits on ice reduce albedo and accelerate melting.

Constructed Wetland

Engineered systems designed to treat wastewater using natural processes involving vegetation, soil, and microbial activity.

Pathways to Resilience

Local, science-backed actions

Ladakh

  • Reduce black carbon (clean transport & stoves).
  • GLOF early-warning & lake monitoring.
  • Seasonal storage — ice stupas & reservoirs.

Western Ghats

  • Sewage treatment & waste management near tourist sites.
  • Agroecological practices to limit runoff.
  • Restore riparian buffers & enforce mining rules.
