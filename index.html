A Tale of Two Crises

Ice and Water Under Threat in India

Ladakh's Melting Glaciers

The Himalayan glaciers — Asia’s “water towers” — are shrinking fast. Ladakh’s Parkachik Glacier is now retreating faster than ever, putting agriculture and water security on the edge.

  • Retreat speed jumped from 2 m/year (1971–1999) to 20 m/year (2015–2021) — a 10× acceleration Wadia Institute, 2022.
  • That’s like losing the length of two school buses of ice every year.
  • Black carbon deposits from diesel and biomass burning lower the glacier’s albedo (reflectivity), making it absorb more heat.

Glacier Retreat Rate

  • Rapid melt forms unstable proglacial lakes — Parkachik could form up to 3 new lakes, increasing GLOF (Glacial Lake Outburst Flood) risk.
  • Ladakh gets up to 85% of its water from glacial melt — reduced flow means agriculture collapse and drinking water scarcity.

Water Dependency

Karnataka's Polluted Western Ghats

The Western Ghats — a UNESCO heritage site — are facing toxic threats from pollution, mining, and deforestation, choking rivers like the Nethravathi and Sharavathi.

  • Microplastics in Nethravathi & Sharavathi — mainly fibers & fragments — enter the food chain via bioaccumulation IISc Study, 2021.
  • Nutrient runoff (nitrogen, phosphorus) causes algal blooms and oxygen depletion — classic eutrophication.

Major Sources of River Pollution

  • Oxygen depletion kills fish and disrupts aquatic ecosystems.
  • Mining & deforestation increase landslide and flood risk.
  • Endemic species are at risk — once gone, they’re gone forever.

Pathways to Resilience

For Ladakh's Glaciers

  • Reduce black carbon: stricter vehicle emission laws.
  • Install early warning systems for GLOFs.
  • Support ice stupa projects to store winter meltwater.

For Karnataka's Rivers

  • Build modern sewage treatment plants.
  • Promote sustainable, low-runoff agriculture.
  • Enforce bans on illegal mining & deforestation.
